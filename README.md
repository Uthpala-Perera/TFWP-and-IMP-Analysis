# TFWP & IMP Analysis Dashboard

## Overview
This project analyzes Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) and International Mobility Program (IMP) data in Canada using an interactive Power BI dashboard.  
The goal is to identify trends in employment, occupation distribution, and regional workforce patterns over time.

---

## Tools & Technologies
- SQL Server  
- T-SQL  
- Power BI  
- Data Cleaning & Transformation  
- Data Visualization  

---

## Key Features
- Analysis of **11M+ total occupation records**  
- Yearly and cumulative job trend analysis (2015–2024)  
- Occupation-wise job distribution (Top occupations)  
- Province/Territory-level workforce analysis  
- Quarterly and monthly job trend breakdown  
- Growth rate and year-over-year change analysis  
- Interactive filtering by year and region

## Key Insights

### Workforce Overview
- Over **11 million occupation records** analyzed  
- Around **2 million jobs recorded in the most recent year**  
- Overall growth of **~246% from 2015 to 2024**, showing strong expansion  

### Trends Over Time
- Steady increase in total jobs over the years, with noticeable growth after 2020  
- Cumulative job totals show a **consistent upward trend**  
- Slight **negative year-over-year change (-0.19)** in the latest period, indicating stabilization  

### Occupation Insights
- A small number of occupations dominate total job counts  
- “Other occupations” category represents a significant portion, indicating diversity in roles  
- Top occupations maintain relatively stable demand over time  

### Regional Insights
- **Ontario contributes the largest share (~39%)** of total jobs  
- Followed by **British Columbia and Quebec**  
- Smaller provinces contribute less but show gradual growth  

### Time-Based Patterns
- Quarterly trends show uneven distribution, with higher activity in certain quarters  
- Monthly trends highlight occasional spikes, suggesting seasonal demand
